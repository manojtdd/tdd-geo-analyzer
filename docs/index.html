What is GEO Analyzer?

GEO Analyzer by TripleDart is a free Chrome extension that scores any public webpage for Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) — the practice of making your content visible and citable in AI-powered search engines like ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, and Claude.

Unlike traditional SEO, GEO focuses on how AI models read, understand, and decide to surface your content. The extension evaluates 36 signals across 6 categories and gives you a 0–100 score with a letter grade and specific, actionable recommendations.

Why GEO matters

AI search engines don't rank pages the traditional way. They read the full content, extract key facts, evaluate credibility signals, and decide whether to include your content in their generated answers. A page can rank #1 on Google but still be completely ignored by AI engines — because it lacks structured data, author signals, or direct-answer formatting.

GEO Analyzer tells you exactly what to fix so your content gets cited by AI.

🔒
100% private by design. All 36 analysis checks run entirely inside your browser. The content of the page you're analyzing is never sent to TripleDart's servers or any third-party service.

Analyzing a Page

What you can analyze

GEO Analyzer works on any regular website — blog posts, landing pages, product pages, documentation, news articles, local business pages, etc. Navigate to the page in Chrome, click the extension icon, then click Analyze Page.

⚠️
Cannot analyze: Chrome's own pages (chrome://), the Chrome Web Store, extension pages, local files (file://), PDFs, or pages behind a login wall. A clear error message is shown if you try.

Re-analyzing after changes

Results are cached for 30 minutes per URL. After editing your page's content or code, click the Re-run button (↺) next to the score to clear the cache and run a fresh analysis immediately.

What the extension reads

  • All visible text content (headings, paragraphs, lists, tables)
  • HTML structure (H1–H6 tags, image alt text, internal/external links)
  • Meta tags (description, Open Graph, Twitter Card, canonical, viewport)
  • JSON-LD schema markup (<script type="application/ld+json">)
  • Performance metrics (Largest Contentful Paint via PerformanceObserver)
  • Page URL and protocol (HTTPS check)

Understanding Your Score

How the score is calculated

Each of the 36 rules has a point value (1–5 pts). When a check passes, you earn the full points. A partial pass earns 50% of the points. A fail earns 0. The total is scaled to 100:

Score = round((earned points / total possible points) × 100)

The maximum possible raw points across all 36 rules is 100, so the formula maps directly.

The 6 Categories

The 36 checks are grouped into 6 categories, each targeting a different dimension of AI-readiness. The points are weighted to reflect how much each factor influences AI engine decisions.

Issues & Passing Tabs

Issues tab

Lists every check that failed or partially passed, sorted by priority: full failures appear first (highest impact), followed by partial passes. Within each tier, rules are sorted by point value — so the top of the Issues list is always where your biggest gains are.

Each issue shows:

  • Rule name — what was checked
  • Category tag — which area it belongs to
  • Point value — how many points you'd gain by fixing it
  • Detail message — a specific description of what was found and how to fix it

Passing tab

Lists all checks that fully passed, with the detail message confirming what was found (e.g., "Author attribution found on page"). Use this to confirm optimizations are working after making changes.

💡
Prioritization tip: Focus on full failures (red) before partial passes (yellow). A full failure gives you the entire point value when fixed; a partial pass only upgrades you from 50% to 100%.

Content Quality & Depth — 8 Rules (25 pts)

These checks evaluate whether your page has enough substance for AI engines to understand, trust, and cite its content.

Rule Pts Pass condition How to fix
Sufficient word count 4 800+ words Expand thin content with more explanation, examples, and supporting detail. Aim for 1,000–2,000 words for key pages. Partial pass at 400–799 words.
Factual claims & statistics 4 5+ numbers/statistics in body text Add specific data points: percentages, years, dollar figures, study results. AI models strongly prefer content with citable, concrete facts. Partial at 2–4 stats.
FAQ or question-based structure 3 FAQPage schema + 3+ question headings Add a FAQ section with "What is…", "How to…", "Why does…" headings. Implement FAQPage JSON-LD schema with mainEntity Q&A pairs. Partial pass if schema or headings present (not both).
Paragraph depth 3 Average 80+ words per paragraph Expand short, thin paragraphs. Each paragraph should develop an idea fully. Avoid one-sentence or two-sentence sections. Partial at 40–79 words average.
No thin or duplicate content 3 Less than 10% repeated sentences Remove repeated boilerplate blocks, templated disclaimers, or copied sentences. Each paragraph should say something unique. Partial at 10–25% repetition.
Meta description 3 150–160 characters Write a compelling <meta name="description"> of exactly 150–160 characters. Summarize what the page is about in plain language. Partial at 50–119 chars or over 160 chars.
Images with descriptive alt text 3 90%+ of images have alt attributes Add meaningful alt="" attributes to every image that conveys information. Describe what the image shows, not "image of" or filename. Partial if 50–89% have alt text.
Internal links for content depth 2 5+ internal links on the page Link naturally to related content on your site. This signals topical authority and helps AI understand your content graph. Partial at 2–4 internal links.

E-E-A-T Signals — 5 Rules (20 pts)

Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness. These signals tell AI whether the content comes from a credible, identifiable source.

RulePtsPass conditionHow to fix
Author byline present 4 Author name detected on page Add a visible "By [Author Name]" attribution near the article title or at the top of the content. Use article:author meta tag and/or Person schema for stronger signals.
Author bio or credentials 3 Author bio section detected Add a brief bio block below the article: name, title/role, and relevant expertise. Include a phrase like "John is a…" or "With 10 years of experience in…". Partial if author found but no bio detected.
Publication / last-updated date 3 Parseable date, updated within 6 months Add <meta property="article:modified_time" content="2025-01-15"> and include a visible "Last updated" date on the page. Partial if 7–18 months old. Fail if over 18 months or no date found.
Citations to authoritative sources 5 3+ links to .gov, .edu, or research journals Link directly to government data, academic research, peer-reviewed studies, or established industry reports. These are the highest-trust citations for AI. Partial at 1–2 authoritative links.
Brand / org entity in schema 3 Organization / Brand schema with name property Add an Organization JSON-LD block with at minimum @type, name, and url. This tells AI who publishes the content. Partial if schema exists but is missing the name field.
External outbound links 2 3+ external links to outside domains Link to supporting sources, reference materials, and relevant external resources. This signals research depth and helps AI place your content in a broader knowledge context. Partial at 1–2 external links.

Structured Data & Markup — 7 Rules (20 pts)

Machine-readable signals that AI crawlers can parse directly, regardless of how the page is visually presented.

RulePtsPass conditionHow to fix
Schema.org JSON-LD present 4 At least 1 JSON-LD <script> block found Add at minimum an Article or WebPage JSON-LD block inside <script type="application/ld+json">. This is the foundation — many other schema rules depend on it.
Schema type matches content 4 Schema @type matches detected content pattern Use Article/BlogPosting for articles, Product for product pages, LocalBusiness for local pages. Mismatches confuse AI about what the page is about.
FAQPage schema with Q&A pairs 3 FAQPage schema with 3+ mainEntity Q&A pairs Add a FAQPage JSON-LD block with a mainEntity array of at least 3 Question/Answer pairs. AI engines extract FAQ schema directly for featured answers. Partial if fewer than 3 Q&A pairs.
Open Graph meta tags 3 og:title, og:description, og:image all present Add all three: og:title, og:description, and og:image in the <head>. Partial if 1–2 are present. The detail message lists which are missing.
Twitter/X Card meta tag 2 twitter:card meta tag present Add <meta name="twitter:card" content="summary_large_image"> along with twitter:title and twitter:description.
Canonical URL tag 2 <link rel="canonical"> present Add <link rel="canonical" href="https://example.com/your-page"> in the <head>. Prevents AI engines from seeing multiple versions of the same content as duplicates.
BreadcrumbList schema 2 BreadcrumbList JSON-LD block found Add a BreadcrumbList schema listing the page's location in your site hierarchy. Helps AI understand topical context (e.g., Blog → Category → Article).

Formatting & Scannability — 7 Rules (15 pts)

AI models extract structured information far more reliably from well-formatted content. These rules check whether your page is organized for both human readers and AI parsers.

RulePtsPass conditionHow to fix
Single unique H1 tag 3 Exactly 1 H1 element on the page Every page should have exactly one H1 that states the primary topic. Zero H1s = AI can't determine the page's main subject. Multiple H1s = AI confusion about which topic is primary.
H2/H3 heading structure 3 3+ H2 sections Break your content into logical sections with H2 headings. AI uses headings as topic anchors for extracting specific answers. Add H3 subsections for longer sections. Partial at 1–2 H2s.
Bullet and numbered lists 2 2+ lists (combined ul + ol) Use <ul> for unordered items and <ol> for steps/ranked items. Lists are easily extracted by AI for structured answers. Partial at exactly 1 list.
Callouts or highlighted key points 2 Callout boxes or 5+ bold elements Add blockquote callouts, highlight boxes, or tip/note panels for key takeaways. Bold 5+ key phrases throughout the content. AI surfaces prominently formatted information more readily.
Table of contents 2 TOC detected (for pages 1,000+ words) Add a linked table of contents near the top of long-form content. Not required for pages under 1,000 words (auto-passes). TOCs help AI navigate long documents and identify section topics.
Reading level ≤ Grade 10 3 Flesch-Kincaid grade level ≤ 10 Use shorter sentences (under 20 words), simpler words, and active constructions. The Flesch-Kincaid formula measures sentence length × syllable complexity. Partial at grade 11–13. Fail above 13.
Comparison tables 3 1+ HTML table(s) present (for 800+ word pages) Add <table> elements for comparisons, feature lists, pricing, or side-by-side data. HTML tables give AI structured data to extract. Auto-passes for short pages (<800 words).

Conversational & AI-Friendly — 5 Rules (12 pts)

These checks evaluate how well your content is structured to match the conversational, question-and-answer nature of AI search interactions.

RulePtsPass conditionHow to fix
Direct answers at section starts 3 60%+ of sections open with a direct statement Lead each section with a direct answer to the implied question the heading poses — before providing context or caveats. AI extracts leading sentences for featured snippets. Partial at 30–59%. Fail below 30%.
Question-style headings 2 3+ headings that start with What, How, Why, When, etc. Rewrite at least 3 section headings as questions that mirror how users ask AI. E.g., "Benefits" → "What are the benefits of X?" Partial at 1–2 question headings.
Active voice 3 Low passive voice usage (under 1.5% passive markers) Rewrite passive constructions ("was found by", "is used by") as active voice ("we found", "users use"). Active sentences are shorter and more direct — AI summarizes them more accurately. Partial at 1.5–3%.
Inline definitions of key terms 2 5+ definition phrases (e.g., "X is defined as…", "X means…", "X refers to…") Define technical terms and acronyms in context the first time they appear. Phrases like "GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) is…" help AI build entity relationships. Partial at 2–4 definitions.
Low jargon density 2 Under 4% complex/jargon words Replace unnecessary jargon with plain-language equivalents. Technical terms are fine when defined, but avoid obscure vocabulary for its own sake. AI comprehends and summarizes plain language more reliably. Partial at 4–8%.

Technical & Freshness — 4 Rules (8 pts)

Baseline technical hygiene and content freshness signals. These are necessary but lower-weighted — fixing them alone won't dramatically move your score, but missing them signals neglect to AI engines.

RulePtsPass conditionHow to fix
HTTPS protocol 1 Page URL starts with https:// Obtain and install an SSL certificate. Most hosts (Netlify, Vercel, Cloudflare, AWS) provide free SSL. HTTPS is a trust prerequisite — AI engines deprioritize or skip HTTP sources.
Core Web Vitals — LCP under 2.5s 2 Largest Contentful Paint < 2,500ms Optimize your largest visible element (usually a hero image or heading). Use lazy loading, serve WebP images, and eliminate render-blocking resources. Partial at 2.5–4s. Fail above 4s.
Mobile viewport meta tag 2 <meta name="viewport"> present Add <meta name="viewport" content="width=device-width, initial-scale=1"> to your <head>. Most CMS platforms include this by default. Mobile-friendliness is a base requirement for AI crawling.
Content updated within 12 months 3 Date signal found and within the last 12 months Update your content regularly and publish the date via article:modified_time meta tag or dateModified in Article schema. AI engines deprioritize content older than 1–2 years. Partial at 13–24 months old.

Export to Google Sheets

After analyzing a page, you can export the full results to a new Google Spreadsheet in your own Google Drive — perfect for sharing with clients, tracking improvements over time, or team audits.

What gets exported

Each export creates a new spreadsheet with 2 tabs:

  • Summary tab — Page URL, overall score and grade, analyzed timestamp, earned/total points, and a score for each of the 6 categories with a color-coded status bar
  • Rule Details tab — All 36 rules in a table: rule name, category, status (Pass / Partial / Fail), points earned vs possible, and the specific detail message

How to export

Click "Export to Sheets"

After a page analysis completes, click the green Export to Sheets button.

Sign in with Google

If you haven't signed in yet, a Google OAuth popup will appear. Choose your Google account and grant the extension permission to create spreadsheets. This only needs to be done once.

Open the spreadsheet

The extension creates the spreadsheet and shows an Open Sheet ↗ link. Click it to open your new Google Sheet. The link persists between sessions.

ℹ️
Each export creates a new spreadsheet. If you want to track a page over multiple audits, rename the sheet with a date. A future version may support updating an existing sheet.

Privacy note

Your analysis data goes directly from your browser to Google's servers — it never passes through TripleDart's servers. The spreadsheet is created in your own Google Drive and is subject to Google's Privacy Policy. TripleDart never stores your Google OAuth token.

Copy Score Summary

Click the Copy button (clipboard icon) in the results panel to copy a plain-text summary of the current analysis to your clipboard. The copied text includes:

  • Page URL and title
  • Overall score, grade, and earned points
  • Per-category scores
  • Top issues (fails + partial passes) with detail messages

Paste it directly into Slack, Notion, a client email, or any other tool.

Privacy & Permissions

GEO Analyzer is designed with a strict privacy-first approach. Here's exactly what each permission is used for:

  • 🔵
    activeTab — Grants access to the current tab only when you click "Analyze Page". The extension cannot access any other tab or browser in the background.
  • 🔵
    scripting — Allows the extension to inject the content script that reads the page's DOM structure for analysis. Used only when you actively click Analyze.
  • 🔵
    storage — Stores your GEO results in a local cache for up to 30 minutes (to avoid re-running the same analysis), and saves your Google Spreadsheet ID so the "Open Sheet" link persists. All data stays on your device.
  • 🔵
    identity — Used only for the Google Sheets export feature. Initiates a Google OAuth 2.0 sign-in flow via Chrome's built-in identity API. The token is held in memory only — never written to disk.
  • 🔵
    sheets.googleapis.com / www.googleapis.com — The only two external hosts the extension can contact. Used exclusively for creating and writing Google Spreadsheets when you export, and for fetching Google account info for the sign-in display.
